Mojito Recipe

A Mojito is a refreshing Cuban cocktail made with white rum, fresh mint leaves, lime juice, sugar, and soda water. It’s typically served over ice, with muddled mint and lime providing a crisp, citrusy, and slightly sweet flavor. Perfect for warm weather, it’s known for its balance of sweetness, tartness, and herbal notes.

Ingredients

  • Fresh mint leaves (10-12 leaves)
  • Lime (Half, cut into wedges)
  • White sugar (2 teaspoons)
  • White rum (1.5-2 ounces)
  • Soda Water (to top off)
  • Ice cubes (as needed)

Steps

  1. In a sturdy glass or shaker, add the mint leaves, lime wedges, and sugar.
  2. Gently muddle (press) with a muddler or the back of a spoon to release the lime juice and mint oils. Be careful not to tear the mint leaves too much.
  3. Add ice cubes to the glass, almost to the top.
  4. Fill the rest of the glass with soda water
  5. Use a spoon or straw to stir lightly, ensuring the ingredients mix well without losing carbonation.
  6. Garnish with a sprig of fresh mint or a lime wheel for an elegant finish.
